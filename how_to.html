Analyzing Deals
↟
↡

Go to the top-right menu bar and click on calculator to analyze your deals.

In this section, there will be all the information you need to know to analyze deals using the rental property calculator.

A note before we even start is that the use of this calculator signifies that you have agreed to use this calculator just for informational and educational purposes. This is not financial advice. The numbers above may or may not reflect actual returns. RealEst or me will not be responsible for decision taken dependent on this calculator. Always seek advice before making any decision.

Report Name

All you have to do is assign a random name to the report that will be produced after you have plugged in the numbers. An example is "Report 1".

Address Information

Enter in the address of the property that you are analyzing. This will be dispayed on the report.

Property Details

Enter in the number of bedrooms and bathrooms your prperty has. Also, enter in the square footage of your property. This is all great information that should be collected or considered by the investor when buying a property. This is optional though. These will be added in the report to gain in-depth insight on the property.

Picture

Upload a picture of the property. It's completely optional. It will be in the report produced by the calculator but not in the pdf report.

Purchase Information

Enter in the purchase price and the closing costs of the loan. Click on the words "Purchase Price" and "Closing Costs" in the calculator to get some information as to what they are.

A Brief description of the Closing Costs is that they are fees paid when a loan is taken. Usually the seller pays them but it can/may be negotiated.

Loan Information

Press the button shown above only if you have taken out a loan to purchase the property and then enter the information that is shown below. If you have paid all cash, skip this section.

If you have taken out a loan, then you will be shown the section in the picture shown above. You'll now have to

Enter the down payment, the amount of money paid out of pocket for the loan.
Enter the interest rate of the loan, the annual interest rate for the loan
Enter the Loan Term, how long the loan will last for. This helps us calculate your monthly mortgage payment.
Enter the Loan Points, an upront fee charged when the loan is taken. One point = One percent of the loan.

Click on the button shown above if you have paid fully in cash and there is no need to enter loan information.

Income

Enter in the rent that you earn from the property. Click the "Add new Income" button if you have other sources of income that generate revene for the property. Examples could be a vending machine or a laundry facility.

Expenses

Enter in all the expenses of the property here and press the "Add new Expense" button if you have other expenses that take out from the property's revenue.

Rehab Information

If you are rehabbing your property click the button on right shown in the picture above.

The above picture shows the information you have to add if you decide to rehab your property.
Enter the ARV (After repair value) of your property, the expected purchase price of your property after the rehab has been completed.
Enter in the costs of the rehab too.

Annual Property Growth

Enter in the percentage at which you think your property value will appreciate annually. This percentage depends on your area. If you don't know anything about it though, 2 percent is an average and safe guess.
